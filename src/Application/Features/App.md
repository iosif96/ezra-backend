# App description:

"Ezra" is a frictionless AI passenger assistant for airports, accessible instantly via WhatsApp, Messenger or Telegram — no app download, no account, no friction. Passengers activate it by scanning a QR code or tapping an NFC sticker placed throughout the terminal. One tap, and help is already there.
Ezra speaks your language automatically, accepts voice messages, and can identify your location from a single photo. Send a picture of your boarding pass and it knows your name, flight, gate, and seat before you type a word. If your flight changes, Ezra messages you first. If you need a person, it hands you off seamlessly. If there's an emergency, it alerts staff instantly.
Behind every conversation sits the "Passenger Control Tower" — a live operations dashboard that shows airport staff what passengers are asking right now, where frustration is building, and where problems are emerging before anyone reports them. Sentiment is monitored in real time. Anomalies trigger automatic alerts.
After your flight boards, Ezra asks how it went for feedback.
The LLM responds in one chunk, but the chunk is split into separate messages in order to be more human like.
Each conversation is evaluated in order to create a conversation tone progression over time and stress index.
If the passenger has time, Ezra slips in commercial suggestions. Proactive notifications can also be sent through the chat (boarding starts, gate changed, ...)
Wheelchair or other forms of special assistance (or emergencies) can be requested through the chat.
Ezra can also start group chats with the whole family upon request.
The agent will be powered by Claude.
Every airport has a Control Tower for planes. Ezra is the first one built for passengers.
